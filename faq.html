Products & Ingredients

How is 10% niacinamide different from what other brands sell?

Most niacinamide serums on the market are dosed at 2–5%. The clinical research supporting visible pore reduction, sebum control, and hyperpigmentation fading consistently uses concentrations of 4% and above — with 10% being the most studied effective dose.

Brands use lower concentrations because it reduces formulation costs while still allowing them to list niacinamide as an active ingredient. LaLume uses 10% because that's the dose the research actually supports. We publish the concentration because if we didn't, you'd have no way of knowing whether a product was dosed to work.

Are LaLume products suitable for sensitive skin?

Most of our products are well-tolerated by sensitive skin types, and we've formulated with that in mind. The Niacinamide Serum includes Panthenol (Vitamin B5) and Allantoin specifically to support the skin barrier and reduce reactivity.

Some people experience mild, temporary flushing in the first 1–2 weeks of using the Niacinamide Serum — this is a known response to niacinamide at clinical concentrations and resolves on its own. It's not an allergic reaction.

One honest contraindication: if you have a known niacin allergy, the Niacinamide Serum is not for you. For everyone else, the 30-day guarantee means you can try it with no financial risk.

Can I use the Niacinamide Serum and Copper Peptide Serum at the same time?

Yes — but we recommend splitting them between AM and PM rather than layering at the same time. Use the Niacinamide Serum in the morning (it pairs well with SPF) and the Copper Peptide Serum in the evening when your skin does most of its repair work.

Copper peptides are most stable when not combined with high-concentration acids (like AHAs or BHAs), but niacinamide doesn't interfere with them. The AM/PM split is simply the most effective way to use both.

Is the LED Therapy Mask safe for home use?

Yes, for most people. The wavelengths used — red (630–700nm) and blue (415–445nm) — are non-ionizing, non-thermal, and have been used in clinical settings for decades. Home-use LED devices operate at lower intensities than professional clinic devices, which makes them safe for regular use without professional supervision.

The mask is not recommended for: people with photosensitive skin conditions (such as lupus or porphyria), people currently taking photosensitizing medications (some antibiotics, certain acne medications, specific supplements), people with epilepsy (due to flashing light), or anyone who has had eye surgery in the past 6 months. Consult your doctor if you're unsure.

Are your products vegan and cruelty-free?

All LaLume products are cruelty-free — we do not test on animals at any stage of production, and we do not sell in markets that require animal testing by law.

Our formulas are vegan with one exception: the Gold Collagen Eye Patches contain Marine Collagen derived from fish. All other products in our range are 100% vegan.

Do your products contain fragrance?

No. None of our products contain added fragrance, synthetic or natural. Fragrance is one of the leading causes of skin sensitization and contact dermatitis, and it provides no functional benefit to the skin. We don't include it.

This means our products have a mild, neutral scent from their active ingredients — which is exactly as it should be.

Where are LaLume products made?

LaLume is a Canadian brand based in British Columbia. Our products are formulated and manufactured in Canada, which means they meet Health Canada's Natural Health Product regulations — some of the strictest product safety standards in the world.

Routines & Usage

In what order should I layer LaLume products?

Morning routine: Cleanser → Niacinamide Clarity Serum → Moisturizer → SPF (always last)

Evening routine: Cleanser → Copper Peptide Renewal Serum → Moisturizer

With tools: Apply the Jade Roller or Gua Sha after your serum and before your moisturizer. Use the LED Therapy Mask after cleansing, over clean skin (you can apply serum first and use the mask over it).

Eye Patches: Apply after cleansing, before serum. Leave on 15–20 minutes, remove, then continue your routine.

Can I use the serums during pregnancy?

We recommend consulting your healthcare provider or dermatologist before using any active skincare during pregnancy.

Specifically: the Gold Collagen Eye Patches contain Retinyl Palmitate (a mild form of Vitamin A), which is generally advised against during pregnancy. The Niacinamide and Copper Peptide Serums don't contain retinoids, but we still recommend checking with your doctor since recommendations can vary based on your individual situation.

How long before I see results?

Honest answer: it depends on the product and your skin.

Niacinamide Serum: Most people notice improved texture and pore appearance in 4–6 weeks of consistent daily use. Weeks 1–2 often show little change — this is normal. Stick with it.

Copper Peptide Serum: Collagen stimulation is a slower process. Meaningful results typically appear at 6–8 weeks, with continued improvement over 3–6 months.

Gold Eye Patches: Visible depuffing and hydration after each use. Cumulative collagen support shows over several weeks of regular use.

LED Therapy Mask: Some immediate improvement in skin luminosity. Anti-aging and acne benefits build over 4–8 weeks of consistent use (3–4 sessions/week).

Jade Ritual Set: Immediate benefits (puffiness reduction, improved circulation). Cumulative benefit with consistent daily use.

Can I use the Jade Roller every day?

Yes — daily use is safe and encouraged. The jade roller improves lymphatic drainage and circulation, reduces morning puffiness, and helps serums absorb more evenly. These benefits are cumulative with consistent use.

Clean your roller regularly — at minimum once a week, ideally after each use. Rinse with warm water and a gentle soap, then dry thoroughly. A dirty roller can transfer bacteria to your skin.

Avoid using on active acne cysts or broken skin, as rolling can spread bacteria. On those areas, skip or use gentle pressure only.
