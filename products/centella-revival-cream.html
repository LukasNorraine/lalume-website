What it does

The Centella Revival Cream is a barrier-first moisturizer built for skin that feels dry, tight, dull, or reactive — regardless of what you've tried before. Centella Asiatica (Cica) has one of the strongest bodies of evidence in cosmetic dermatology for calming inflammation, supporting wound healing, and stimulating collagen synthesis at the dermis level.

We combined it with ceramides, squalane, and niacinamide to address the three most common barrier failure points: missing lipids, impaired water retention, and chronic low-grade inflammation. Most moisturizers address one. This addresses all three.

What it doesn't do

It won't replace SPF. It won't reverse decades of sun damage overnight. What it will do — within two to four weeks of daily use — is measurably restore barrier integrity and reduce the feeling of tight, reactive, dehydrated skin.

Seven ingredients. Each chosen to address a specific point of barrier failure — not to fill a label or justify a price.

Centella Asiatica Extract (Cica) PRIMARY ACTIVE

Standardized to madecassoside and asiaticoside content — the two triterpenes with the most clinical evidence for collagen stimulation, wound healing acceleration, and anti-inflammatory activity. We use extract, not powder, for better bioavailability.

Ceramide NP + AP + EOP BARRIER

Three ceramide types that mirror the natural composition of a healthy skin barrier. Ceramides are the lipid "mortar" between skin cells — when depleted, the barrier leaks water and lets irritants in. Replacing them is the fastest way to restore function.

Squalane EMOLLIENT

Plant-derived squalane is skin-identical — structurally similar to the squalene your skin naturally produces. Non-comedogenic, stable, and absorbs without residue. Provides the emollient layer that keeps the ceramides in place.

Niacinamide (5%) TONE + BARRIER

At 5%, supports ceramide synthesis from within and reduces transepidermal water loss. Works synergistically with the Centella extract to calm redness and improve barrier recovery speed.

Sodium Hyaluronate HYDRATION

Low molecular weight for epidermal penetration. Binds water and maintains the hydrated environment the Centella actives need to function. Prevents the tight, dehydrated feeling that compromised skin defaults to.

Full INCI list: Aqua, Centella Asiatica Extract, Glycerin, Squalane, Cetearyl Alcohol, Ceramide NP, Ceramide AP, Ceramide EOP, Niacinamide, Sodium Hyaluronate, Panthenol, Allantoin, Carbomer, Phenoxyethanol, Ethylhexylglycerin.

01
Cleanse and apply serums first

Moisturizer goes last in your routine — after cleansing, toning, and any active serums. The cream seals in the actives and prevents water loss after application.

02
Apply to slightly damp skin

Slightly damp skin (not wet) allows the ceramides and hyaluronate to bind water more effectively. Apply within 60 seconds of any serum or facial mist.

03
Use a pea-sized amount for full face

Warm between fingertips and press gently into skin. A little goes further than you expect — start with less and add more if needed. More product does not mean more barrier repair.

04
Morning: follow with SPF

This cream provides no UV protection. In your morning routine, always finish with SPF 30+. UV is the leading cause of barrier damage — protecting it matters as much as repairing it.

Routine pairing

Works as the moisturizer step in both AM and PM routines. Pairs with the Niacinamide Clarity Serum (AM) and Copper Peptide Renewal Serum (PM). The 5% niacinamide in this cream complements the 20% niacinamide serum without over-dosing — the mechanisms are slightly different at each concentration.
