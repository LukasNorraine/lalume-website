What it does

The Copper Peptide Renewal Serum delivers GHK-Cu — one of the most extensively studied anti-aging actives in cosmetic science — at a concentration that actually matters. Most anti-aging serums contain copper peptides as a marketing ingredient, often below 0.5% where clinical data shows minimal effect.

We formulated at the concentration range where peer-reviewed studies show statistically significant improvements in collagen density, skin firmness, and fine line reduction. We also combined GHK-Cu with Argireline, which works through a complementary pathway to reduce expression-related lines.

What it doesn't do

It won't erase decades of sun damage in a week. What it will do — with consistent daily use — is measurably slow and partially reverse the collagen loss your skin is experiencing right now. That's a meaningful claim, backed by evidence.

Who it's NOT for

  • Use alongside high-dose ascorbic acid (vitamin C) — copper and vitamin C can chelate and reduce each other's efficacy; separate to AM/PM
  • Anyone expecting overnight results — collagen remodeling is measured in weeks, not days
  • Skin that relies heavily on strong AHAs nightly — give copper peptides their own window to work

Clinical reference: Pickart L. (2008). The human tri-peptide GHK and tissue remodeling. Journal of Biomaterials Science, 19(8), 969–988.

Five ingredients. Each chosen because the evidence said so — not because it photographs well or sounds luxurious.

GHK-Cu (Copper Tripeptide-1) PRIMARY ACTIVE

The copper peptide with the strongest body of evidence for collagen I and III stimulation. Studies show it also upregulates TIMP-1 and TIMP-2, enzymes that inhibit collagen breakdown. This is the ingredient this serum is built around.

Amino Acid Complex SUPPORT

A blend of skin-identical amino acids — glycine, proline, hydroxyproline — that serve as building blocks for collagen synthesis. Provides the raw materials GHK-Cu signals the skin to use.

Sodium Hyaluronate HYDRATION

Low molecular weight HA that penetrates the stratum corneum and binds water within the dermis. Plumps fine lines from within while the peptides work deeper.

Argireline (Acetyl Hexapeptide-3) EXPRESSION LINES

Inhibits the neurotransmitter signals that cause repetitive muscle contractions, reducing the depth of expression lines over time. Works differently from GHK-Cu — complementary, not redundant.

Vitamin E (Tocopherol) ANTIOXIDANT

A fat-soluble antioxidant that neutralizes free radicals in the lipid-rich layers of the skin. Protects existing collagen from oxidative degradation while the peptides stimulate new production.

Full INCI list: Aqua, Glycerin, Sodium Hyaluronate, Copper Tripeptide-1 (GHK-Cu), Glycine, Proline, Hydroxyproline, Acetyl Hexapeptide-3, Tocopherol, Allantoin, Panthenol, Phenoxyethanol, Ethylhexylglycerin.

01
Cleanse thoroughly

Start with a clean, dry face. Remove all SPF, makeup, and daily buildup. The peptides need a clear path — anything sitting on the surface reduces penetration efficiency.

02
Apply 4–5 drops to fingertips

Dispense 4–5 drops and warm between fingertips. Don't apply dropper directly to face — keeps the formula uncontaminated and gives you better distribution control.

03
Press into skin — don't rub

Use gentle pressing motions across cheeks, forehead, jawline, and neck. Pressing drives the serum in more effectively than rubbing. Focus extra time on areas of concern.

04
Allow 60 seconds to absorb

Wait a full minute before applying anything else. If you use a vitamin C product, apply it separately — morning vs. evening — to avoid pH competition between the actives.

05
Moisturize, then SPF in the AM

Follow with your moisturizer. In the morning, finish with SPF 30+. UV exposure degrades collagen faster than any other environmental factor — protecting what you're building is non-negotiable.

Pairing note

Copper peptides work synergistically with retinol (use on alternating nights if new to both), and very well with hyaluronic acid. Avoid combining directly with high-concentration vitamin C or acids in the same application step — pH competition can reduce effectiveness of both.
