The Jade Facial Ritual Set is a force multiplier for your existing skincare routine. The two tools address the foundational issues that even the best serums can't fix on their own: lymphatic congestion and poor product absorption.

Gua sha is not a beauty trend — it is a traditional Chinese medicine technique with a documented history of improving facial circulation and reducing chronic puffiness. The jade stone's natural temperature-retaining properties keep it cool during use, which constricts blood vessels and reduces inflammation while you work.

Material: Natural jade (both pieces)
Best used with: Any LaLume serum for amplified absorption
Ideal routine: Morning, after serum application

Who it's NOT for

  • Active acne cysts or pustules — massage and scraping over active breakouts can spread bacteria and worsen inflammation
  • Broken capillaries or active rosacea flares — the circulation-stimulating effect can aggravate visible redness in these conditions
  • Sunburned, irritated, or broken skin — wait until the skin barrier is fully intact before using either tool

Clinical reference: Kwong KW. (2021). Facial gua sha: A systematic review of the evidence for lymphatic drainage and microcirculation effects. Journal of Integrative Medicine, 19(4), 295–302.

Jade Facial Roller
Dual-ended natural jade roller — larger stone for cheeks, forehead, and neck; smaller stone for under-eye area and nose. The rolling motion promotes lymphatic drainage and distributes applied serum across the skin for even absorption.
Gua Sha Stone
Heart-shaped natural jade gua sha with notched edges for the jawline and neck. The scraping technique creates a controlled micro-trauma that increases local circulation and breaks up fascial adhesions that contribute to a "puffy" resting face.
Technique Guide
Illustrated card with step-by-step directions for both tools — correct pressure, direction, and sequence for lymphatic drainage and facial sculpting.
1
Apply serum first. The tools are most effective when used over a serum. The slight slip helps tools glide and the rolling/scraping motion drives product deeper into the skin.
2
Gua sha first. Start with the gua sha stone. Hold at a 30–45 degree angle. Use gentle, upward and outward strokes from the center of the face toward the hairline and down the neck to the collarbone. This is the drainage pathway.
3
Then jade roller. Switch to the jade roller. Roll upward and outward from the center of the face. Use the small end for the under-eye area — always roll from inner to outer corner toward the temple.
4
Neck and collarbone. Finish by rolling down the neck and across the collarbone. This moves accumulated lymph fluid toward the drainage nodes at the clavicle.
5
Frequency. Morning use is most effective for reducing overnight puffiness. 5–10 minutes daily. Store the jade roller in the fridge for extra cooling benefit and maximum depuffing effect.
