What it does

The Centella Purity Cleanser removes what needs to go — makeup, sunscreen, pollution, excess sebum — without disturbing the acid mantle or stripping the lipids that hold your barrier together. This sounds basic. Most cleansers fail it.

We use amino acid-derived surfactants instead of sulfates. The foaming action is gentler and the rinse-off pH is kept within the skin's natural range. Centella Asiatica extract adds an anti-inflammatory function — so you're not just cleaning, you're actively calming any low-grade irritation your skin accumulates throughout the day.

What it doesn't do

It won't remove heavy waterproof makeup in one pass — use a dedicated oil cleanser or micellar water first if you wear waterproof formulas. It also isn't exfoliating. If you want chemical exfoliation, use a dedicated AHA/BHA toner separately.

Six active ingredients. Nothing that compromises the cleanse, nothing that compromises the skin.

Centella Asiatica Extract PRIMARY ACTIVE

Standardized Cica extract providing madecassoside and asiaticoside. In a cleanser context, these actives work to reduce post-cleanse redness and support the skin's own barrier repair signaling — particularly useful for rosacea-prone and reactive skin types.

Sodium Cocoyl Glutamate SURFACTANT

An amino acid-derived surfactant with a mild, skin-compatible pH. Provides effective cleansing without the barrier disruption associated with sodium lauryl sulfate or sodium laureth sulfate. The mild foaming action removes sebum and environmental residue cleanly.

Panthenol (Vitamin B5) BARRIER SUPPORT

Converts to pantothenic acid on skin contact, supporting barrier function and reducing transepidermal water loss. In a cleanser, it acts as a leave-some-behind active — even in rinse-off products, panthenol has demonstrated measurable barrier support.

Allantoin CALMING

A skin-conditioning agent that promotes cell turnover gently and reduces post-cleanse sensitivity. Particularly effective for skin that typically feels tight or reactive immediately after washing.

Glycerin HUMECTANT

A humectant that counteracts the temporary dehydration effect of surfactant cleansing. Helps maintain the water content of the stratum corneum throughout the wash, reducing the tight post-cleanse feeling common with standard face washes.

01
Wet face with lukewarm water

Hot water raises skin temperature and increases transepidermal water loss. Lukewarm is enough to open pores and activate the surfactants without added stress to the barrier.

02
Apply a small amount and lather

A pea-to-grape-sized amount is plenty for the full face. Work into a gentle lather between palms first, then apply to skin in circular motions. Avoid the eye area.

03
Massage for 30–60 seconds

Give the surfactants time to bind to and lift oil and debris. Rushing reduces effectiveness. Focus on the T-zone, hairline, and jawline where buildup concentrates.

04
Rinse thoroughly with cool water

Cool water to finish helps close pores and the slightly cooler temperature seals the acid mantle. Pat (don't rub) dry with a clean towel and proceed with serum within 60 seconds.

Routine placement

First step — always. In the morning, use once (your skin doesn't accumulate significant debris overnight). In the evening, double cleanse if you've worn SPF or makeup: oil cleanser first to dissolve, then this cleanser to purify. Follow immediately with your serum and moisturizer while skin is still slightly damp.
