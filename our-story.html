The Beginning

We spent thousands. Our skin never changed.

Like most Canadians, we tried everything — department store serums, clinical brands, dermatologist recommendations. Some worked a little. Most worked not at all. The patterns became clear: beautiful packaging, impressive claims, and ingredient lists that barely touched clinical effectiveness.

We started asking why. Why does a $200 serum contain 0.1% niacinamide when research shows 10% is the effective dose? Why do "collagen-boosting" creams not mention that topical collagen molecules are too large to penetrate skin?

The answer, we learned, is that most skincare is built around marketing — not biology.

🔬
The Research

So we went to the science instead.

We spent 18 months in peer-reviewed research before formulating a single product. Dermatology journals, clinical trial data, cosmetic chemistry textbooks. We wanted to understand which ingredients actually work — and at what concentrations.

GHK-Cu (copper peptides) became a focus because the clinical evidence for collagen stimulation is substantial and decades old. Niacinamide at 10% was chosen because that's the dose that shows statistically significant pore reduction. Nothing arbitrary.

We also wanted to understand what doesn't work — so we could stop including it.

🇨🇦
The Mission

Clean formulas. Honest prices. Made for our climate.

LaLume launched with one guiding principle: every product must justify its existence. No ingredient makes it into a formula without a reason, and that reason must be backed by evidence — not tradition or marketing.

We also priced differently. By skipping department store retail markups and investing in formulas instead of advertising, we can offer clinical-grade actives at prices that don't require a second mortgage.

We're based in British Columbia. We understand Canadian skin — the cold winters, the hard water, the barrier stress of our climate. Everything we make accounts for that.
