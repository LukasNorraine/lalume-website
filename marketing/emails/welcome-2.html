Why we publish every ingredient, every concentration

No proprietary blends. No exceptions.

Most skincare brands list ingredients in the order required by law — but the law doesn't require them to tell you the concentration. That's how a serum can say "Vitamin C" on the front and contain 0.5% when the research shows you need 15%.

We decided not to do that. Every LaLume product page lists every active ingredient, exactly why we included it, and what concentration it's at. You can look up the research and verify whether the dose we use is the dose that's been shown to work. We want you to be able to fact-check us.

What's the active ingredient?

And what does the research actually say it does? The ingredient label tells you it's there — it doesn't tell you whether it does what the marketing claims.

At what concentration?

Brands use low concentrations because it reduces formulation costs while still letting them list an ingredient as active. Clinical research uses specific doses. The two aren't always the same. We publish ours so you can compare.

What are the supporting ingredients?

The actives get the spotlight, but barrier-supporting ingredients — like Panthenol and Allantoin — often determine whether you'll actually tolerate a formula. We're specific about these too, because they matter for real-world use.

We answer all three questions on every product page. Every formula, every time. That's the standard we hold ourselves to.
