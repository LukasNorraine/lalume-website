What's actually in the Niacinamide Serum

Every ingredient. Every concentration. No proprietary blends.

Niacinamide — 10%

Niacinamide is a form of Vitamin B3. The clinical studies showing meaningful results — reduced pore appearance, improved skin tone, sebum regulation — used concentrations of 4% and above, with 10% being the most studied dose. Most mass-market products use 2–5% to cut costs while still listing it as an active. We use 10% because that's what works.

Zinc PCA

Zinc PCA is a sebum-regulating compound that works especially well alongside niacinamide. If you have oily or combination skin, this is one of the reasons this serum works better than niacinamide-only formulas. It helps control shine without drying the skin out, and it has antimicrobial properties that support clearer skin over time.

Sodium Hyaluronate (low molecular weight)

Standard hyaluronic acid sits on the skin's surface. Low molecular weight Sodium Hyaluronate penetrates into the deeper layers of the epidermis, delivering hydration where it's actually needed. It's a meaningful distinction that most labels don't bother to specify. We specify it because it matters.

Panthenol (Vitamin B5)

Panthenol converts to Pantothenic Acid in the skin, where it supports the barrier and accelerates repair. It's one of the reasons this serum is well-tolerated even by sensitive skin types — it's actively calming inflammation and supporting recovery while the actives do their work.

Allantoin

Allantoin is a keratolytic and soothing agent. It gently promotes cell turnover — helping the skin shed older surface cells and replacing them with fresh ones. Combined with niacinamide, it contributes to the brighter, more even complexion most people notice by week three or four.

No hidden ingredients. No proprietary blends. You know exactly what you're putting on your face.
