What's actually in the Centella Brightening Serum

Every ingredient. Every concentration. No proprietary blends.

Madagascar Centella Asiatica (Cica)

Centella is the anchor ingredient. It contains asiaticoside, madecassoside, and asiatic acid — triterpenoids that reduce inflammatory cytokines, stimulate collagen synthesis, and accelerate barrier repair. Most serums use generic Centella. We source from Madagascar specifically because it carries a higher concentration of active compounds. The difference shows up in results.

Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)

Niacinamide inhibits melanin transfer to surface skin cells, which is how it fades post-blemish marks and evens tone over time. It also reduces sebum production and strengthens the lipid barrier. It works synergistically with Centella — one calms the inflammation causing redness, the other corrects the visible damage it leaves behind.

Sodium Hyaluronate (low molecular weight)

Standard hyaluronic acid sits on the skin’s surface. Low molecular weight Sodium Hyaluronate penetrates into the deeper layers of the epidermis, delivering sustained hydration where it supports barrier function. It’s a meaningful distinction most labels don’t bother to specify. We specify it because it matters.

Panthenol (Pro-Vitamin B5)

Panthenol converts to pantothenic acid in the skin, where it supports cell repair and moisture retention. It’s particularly effective alongside barrier-repairing actives — it accelerates the recovery process. One of the main reasons this serum is well-tolerated by reactive and sensitive skin types from day one.

Allantoin

Allantoin is a gentle keratolytic and soothing agent. It promotes controlled cell turnover — helping the skin shed older surface cells and replace them with fresh ones. Combined with Centella and Niacinamide, it contributes to the brighter, more even complexion most people notice by week three or four.

No hidden ingredients. No proprietary blends. You know exactly what you’re putting on your face.
